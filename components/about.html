Fake News Detector

Introduction

In the age of internet where information is shareable to the rest of the world with some few clicks, there is no solid way to determine the authenticity of some shared news. Some people take advantage of these facts to spread fake news ensuing chaos among the masses. This problem can be solved by leveraging the current advancements in ML technologies to detect pattern among news articles and the abundance of data that is present everywhere to create an application. This application would cater to the needs of general public on the fly without compromising much on the reliability. Introduction of such an application will help people to curb fake news and stop its propagation at the ground level. It will also help us to create awareness about the seriousness of this issue .

Necessity

Although this issue has been around for quite some time now but the real magnitude of the threat it poses came under spotlight only in recent 3 to 4 years:

  • In the United States, disinformation scandals marred the presidential election in 2016, and questions about what role disinformation played in the election of Donald Trump reverberate today. Based on some clear evidence that a Kremlin-linked organization, Internet Research Agency, spent hundreds of thousands of dollars to fuel toxic political discourse, before and after the election. The current Indian elections also face the same challenges as almost 1 in every 2 people receive fake news of some sort from digital platforms.
  • In the past year, there has been a rise in the cases of mob lynchings across India. The victims in such cases are mainly normal people attacked by mobs fuelled by different rumors over whatsapp, facebook.
  • A major problem is that people directly believe whatever they read on social media. They hardly give it a second thought and even more rarely they go for fact checking.
